Marie Curie - Nobel Prize-winning physicist and chemist

Marie Curie was a remarkable scientist who made groundbreaking contributions to the fields of physics and chemistry. Born on November 7, 1867, in Warsaw, Poland, she became the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and the only person to win Nobel Prizes in multiple scientific disciplines.

Her research on radioactivity led to the discovery of two new elements, polonium and radium. Marie Curie's pioneering work laid the foundation for modern physics and revolutionized the field of medical science. Her discoveries in radiation therapy continue to save lives and have significant applications in cancer treatment.

In recognition of her extraordinary achievements, Marie Curie received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1903 and the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1911. She remains an inspiration to countless scientists, especially women, around the world.
